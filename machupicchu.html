Machu Picchu, often called the "Lost City of the Incas," is a 15th-century citadel built by the Inca emperor Pachacuti. Located at an altitude of 2,430 meters, it remained hidden from the outside world until its discovery in 1911 by Hiram Bingham. The site showcases the Incas' remarkable engineering skills, with terraces, temples, and sophisticated drainage systems. Machu Picchu is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most iconic symbols of Peru, attracting millions of visitors annually with its mystery and breathtaking beauty.
